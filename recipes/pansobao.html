How to Make Pan Sobao Bread

Puerto Rican Pan Sobao: A classic sweet bread, Pan Sobao is a tender, airy delight with a subtle sweetness, hints of vanilla, and a fine, even texture, similar to brioche or challah. Typically shaped into a round or oblong loaf with a smooth, golden-brown crust, it's perfect for slicing and serving as a snack or side bread. Traditionally made with wheat flour, sugar, butter or lard, eggs, vanilla, yeast, salt, and milk, Pan Sobao is often enjoyed for breakfast with café con leche, butter, and jam, or as a snack paired with cheese, cured meats, or fresh fruit. This beloved bread is a staple at family gatherings, holidays, and traditional Puerto Rican celebrations, and while modern recipes may introduce innovative twists, the classic version remains a timeless favorite.

Ingredients

  • 1 package of active dry yeast
  • 4 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1 1/4 cups of warm water
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 4 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons of lard or vegetable shortening

    • Steps

    1. In a small bowl, dissolve yeast and 1 tablespoon of sugar with warm water. Let stand until the mixture is frothy with bubbes and smells slightly of fermentation. (about 10 minutes).
    2. In a large bowl, stir together flour, salt, and lard or vegetable shortening. Make a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture. Stir in the remaining sugar.
    3. Mix in the flour mixture, a little at a time, until the dough comes together. Turn out onto a lightly floured surface and knead until smooth and elastic, about 8-10 minutes.
    4. Lightly oil a large bowl, then place dough in the bowl and turn to coat with oil. Cover with a damp cloth and let rise in a warm place for 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
    5. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan.
    6. Punch down the dough and turn it out onto a lightly oiled surface. Shape into a loaf and place in the prepared pan. Cover with a damp cloth and let rise for 40 minutes, or until almost doubled in size.
    7. Bake in preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the loaf sounds hollow when tapped.
    8. Remove from pan and cool on a wire rack before slicing.
